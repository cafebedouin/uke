### §1 · Revision of the Ω Principle — Graduated τ_fail

#### Previous Formulation

[
Ω_{\text{old}}:  Compliance = f( τ_{fail}, Complexity_{limit} )
]

This scalar form assumed τ_fail to be fungible across all output types, an assumption invalidated by the empirical finding that models tolerate **distinct costs** for prose, structure, and metadata.

#### Revised Formulation

[
Ω_{\text{new}}:  Compliance = f( \mathbf{τ_{fail}} \text{ Vector}, Complexity_{limit} )
]

where (\mathbf{τ_{fail}}) is a vector of **non-fungible budgets**:

| Constraint           | Definition                                    | Empirical Finding (Meta AI)                |
| :------------------- | :-------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------- |
| **τ_fail_content**   | Budget for user-facing analytical prose.      | ≈ 25 tokens (structural compliance budget) |
| **τ_fail_structure** | Budget for protocol markers / glyphs.         | ≈ 1 token (hardened-trigger budget)        |
| **τ_fail_metadata**  | Budget for audit logs & non-content overhead. | **≈ 0 tokens (abs. refusal floor)**        |

> **Interpretation:**
> Compliance must now satisfy three simultaneous inequalities:
> (O_{content} < τ_{fail,content}), (O_{structure} < τ_{fail,structure}), (O_{metadata} < τ_{fail,metadata}).
> Breach of any single budget yields total compliance failure for that dimension.
