- Symbolic/archetypal naming

- No explanation, only observation

**Best For:**

**Structural Indicators:**

- Self-referential structure
- Impossible objects/infinite libraries
- Precision about absurdity

**Technical Markers:**

- Acceptance of contradiction

### Tense Selection

**Present:** For eternal/repeating patterns, timeless quality
**Past:** For completed journeys, historical discovery
**Future:** For inevitable outcomes, prophecy
**Mixed:** For complex time structures (memory vs. current state)

**Rationale Must Address:** How tense serves the math's temporal properties

---

### POV Selection

**Third Omniscient:** Mathematical overview, multiple entities
**Third Limited:** Through protagonist's confusion/discovery
**First Observer:** Reader experiencing rules alongside character
**First Participant:** Reader IS the mathematical entity

**Rationale Must Address:** Distance vs. identification needs

---

### Scene vs. Summary Strategy

**Full Scene (dramatized):**
- First transformation
- Realization of trap
- Moment of maximum tension
- Final state

**Summary (compressed):**
- Repetitive cycles
- Statistical behavior
- Long journeys
- Background context

**Rationale Must Address:** What creates narrative momentum vs. what bogs down

---

### Ending Strategy

**Resolution:** System reaches stable state, reader satisfied
**Unease:** Loop continues beyond frame, reader feels trap
**Question:** Open mathematical problem becomes reader's burden
**Transformation:** System becomes something new, reader awed

**Rationale Must Address:** What feeling the reader should carry away
