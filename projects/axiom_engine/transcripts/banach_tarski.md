**Act I: The Bounded World**

**Act II: The Descent Through Choice**

**Act III: The Resurrection Paradox**
Rigid motions (the most conservative transformations possible - no stretching, no distortion) reassemble the null sets. Two balls appear where one stood. The Measure recognizes them as valid, measurable, full of volume.

**The Revelation:**
Conservation was never violated *from the system's perspective*. What changed was *which framework was doing the accounting*. The Measure couldn't see the intermediate state. The system exploited that blindness.

This is not a story about creation or destruction. It's a story about **existing in the gaps between what different observers can see**.

The first fragment: the Surveyor extends its grid, but the lines dissolve at the edges. No volume registers. "Null," records the Surveyor. The second: again, the grid falters, the interior vanishing into points without extent. Null. The third, fourth, fifth—each a ghost in the ledger, sets of measure zero. The Surveyor steps back. "The Orb is undone," it concludes. "What was whole is now nothing, dispersed into voids." Yet the fragments persist, definite in their membership, real in the archive's logic. In the margins, the fragments' echoes linger, unmeasurable yet essential. The Orbs stand, duplicated, their existence a catalog of incompatibilities. The Surveyor closes its ledger, bewildered by the gap it cannot bridge. The archive expands, but the blindness endures.
