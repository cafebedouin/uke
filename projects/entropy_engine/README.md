## üìñ The Operator‚Äôs Manual (How to Prompt)

Success depends on the "Semantic Mass" of your seed. Words act as gravity wells, and the Engine works harder to escape common concepts.

### Seed Strategies

* **Gravity Well (Single Word):** Use high-gravity words like *"Love"* or *"Home"* to force the Engine to generate massive thrust to escape clich√©.
* **Trajectory Shift (Word + Domain):** Guide the weirdness by adding a flavor. *Example: "Forgiveness, but as geological erosion"*.
* **Counter-Logic (Word + Paradox):** Force the Engine to prove a contradiction. *Example: "Forgiveness, but as economic liquidation"*.

### The Slingshot Rule (WARNING)

**Do not pre-solve the entropy.**

* **‚ùå Bad Seed:** *"A toaster that is actually a dragon."* (This gives the Engine a destination, reducing creativity).
* **‚úÖ Good Seed:** *"A toaster, but viewed as a tactical negotiation asset."* (This gives the Engine a vector, forcing it to invent new logic).

## Troubleshooting

* **Output feels too poetic?** Remind the model of the **Sacrifice** requirement. The goal is *not* beauty; it is conceptual distance.
* **Metaphors are too obvious?** Invoke the **Anti-Salad Protocol**: Ensure the system obeys its own internal rules and isn't just mixing free-floating imagery.
