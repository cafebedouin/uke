### 6. Philosophical Positioning Enhancement (Section VIII) - ~270 words

**Location:** Epistemology subsection under Philosophical Positioning
**Rationale:** Perplexity and others noted need to distinguish from Critical Realism and Agential Realism

**Added content:**
- **vs. Critical Realism (Bhaskar):** Shares commitment to reality beyond appearances but:
  - Focuses on constraint-types not depth ontology
  - Emphasizes actionability over metaphysical architecture
  - Power analysis as epistemic necessity not addition
  
- **vs. Agential Realism (Barad):** Both center power-knowledge but:
  - DR maintains clearer ontological commitments
  - Provides operational decision tools
  - More prescriptive about classification and response
  
- **Common ground:** All reject naive empiricism and recognize power in knowledge production
- **DR's position:** Middle ground—more operationally prescriptive than Agential Realism, less metaphysically committed than Critical Realism

**Significance:** Positions the framework clearly in contemporary philosophical landscape. Shows awareness of related work while articulating distinctive contribution.
